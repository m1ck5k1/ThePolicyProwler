# H.R. 8007 SILVER Act — Key Sections & Forensic Notes

## Sec. 1 — Short Title
"System Integrity through Licensed Vault Expansion and Resilience Act" / "SILVER Act"

## Sec. 2 — Findings (The UI Layer)

| Finding | Official Framing | Forensic Read |
|---|---|---|
| (1) | Precious metals require storage near NYC | Establishes the problem to be solved |
| (2) | Geographic concentration = systemic risk | Valid on its face; also the legislative hook |
| (3) | "Liquidity events" underscore need to minimize barriers | Vague reference to real market events (LME nickel 2022?) used to justify deregulation |
| (4) | Existing vault security is "outstanding" | Neutralizes opposition from incumbent vault operators |
| (5) | More vaults = more liquidity and confidence | Generic market efficiency argument |
| **(6)** | **"Lower-cost markets near hubs of precious metals activity and interstate transportation networks"** | **Geographic description matching Idaho / Coeur d'Alene / Union Pacific corridor precisely** |
| (7) | DCOs should have transparent depository selection | Frames forced transparency as public interest |

**Finding 6 is the tell.** No geography is named, but the description — precious metals activity hubs + interstate transportation networks — maps directly to: Idaho (Silver Valley), Nevada (Elko mining corridor), Utah (I-15/I-80 intersection), and the Union Pacific / BNSF freight corridors through the Mountain Time Zone.

## Sec. 3 — Amendments to Commodity Exchange Act (The Kernel)

### §5b(c)(2)(E)(vii) — Systemic Risk Addition
Inserts "geographic concentration of depositories for gold, silver, platinum, and palladium" as an
explicit systemic risk factor DCOs must manage. Makes non-NYC storage a *compliance requirement*,
not just an option.

### §5b(c)(2)(F)(iii) — Depository Approval Mandate (NEW clause)

**Subclause (I) — General:** DCOs must:
- Publish objective/transparent selection criteria
- Provide a formal application process

**Subclause (II) — Selection Factors:** DCOs must assess:
- Geographic diversity
- Competition
- Risk management
- *Storage costs to members* ← commercial pressure on incumbents
- Systemic risk implications

**Subclause (III) — Geographic Requirement:** ← THE OPERATIVE CLAUSE
- Minimum **2 approved depositories per time zone**
- Time zones: Eastern, Central, Mountain, Pacific
- Currently: ~8 Eastern, ~0 Central, **0 Mountain, 0 Pacific**
- Required new approvals: minimum 4 (2 Mountain + 2 Pacific), likely more

### §5b(c)(2)(I)(iv) — Physical Settlement Access Review (NEW)
DCOs must "periodically assess ease of access for market participants with respect to physical
settlement of any commodity, regardless of geographic location within the United States."
This creates ongoing compliance pressure to keep expanding geographic coverage.

### §5b(c)(2)(L)(iii)(V) — Metal Service Provider Application Conditions (NEW)
DCOs must publish conditions for applying and receiving approval as a "metal service provider,
such as a depository for the storage of precious metals." Formalizes the application pathway
that currently doesn't exist in statute.

### §5b(c)(2)(N)(i) — Disclosure Addition
Adds "approval of a metal service provider, such as a depository" to existing disclosure
requirements. Creates public record of approval decisions.
