# H.R. 1 — "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" (OBBBA)
**Public Law:** 119-21
**Source:** https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/1
**House Vote:** July 3, 2025 — 218–214
**Senate Passage:** Via reconciliation / Vote-a-Rama
**CBO Signed Estimate:** July 2025 — $3.4T deficit expansion (dynamic); $4.1T with interest premium
**Total Pages:** 1,116

## Key Sections Referenced in Audits

### Sec. [Rural Health Transformation Fund] — $50 Billion Liquidity Bribe
- **Amount:** $50,000,000,000
- **Stated Purpose:** Rural healthcare modernization and delivery improvement
- **Forensic Finding:** Targeted disbursement to districts of the "Pivot 5" — the five swing votes
  required to reach 218. Functions as a localized liquidity bribe to neutralize blowback from the
  $137 billion Medicaid cuts elsewhere in the bill.
- **Monthly Disbursement:** ~$833 million / **Daily Leak:** ~$27.4 million
- **Audit:** AUD-260222-OBBBA-LOBBYIST-ROI, AUD-260222-OBBBA-ANCESTRY

### Sec. 2.2.14 — "Tax Deduction for Whaling" (Midnight Rider)
- **Provision:** Quintupled the tax deduction for Alaska Native whaling captains ($10,000 → $50,000).
  Added 100% deduction for commercial fishing crew meals.
- **Forensic Finding:** Non-fiscal rider injected during Senate Vote-a-Rama. Classic obfuscated
  payload — anachronistic carve-out buried in a 1,116-page reconciliation bill.
- **Audit:** AUD-260222-OBBBA-ANCESTRY, AUD-260222-OBBBA-MECHANICS

### Sec. [Silencer Tax Repeal] — (Midnight Rider)
- **Provision:** Repeal of the federal excise tax on firearm suppressors.
- **Forensic Finding:** Non-fiscal rider injected during Senate Vote-a-Rama. No relationship to
  budget reconciliation. Injected while chamber defenses were lowered during the late-night session.
- **Audit:** AUD-260222-OBBBA-ANCESTRY, AUD-260222-OBBBA-MECHANICS

### Medicaid Cuts — $137 Billion
- **Amount:** $137,000,000,000 in Medicaid reductions over 10 years
- **Activation:** January 1, 2026
- **Ground Truth:** Clinic closures initiated in OH, IA; MercyOne Ottumwa closing; Iowa projects
  $2 billion in lost healthcare spending over the decade.
- **Audit:** AUD-260222-HUMAN-IMPACT
